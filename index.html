Major Mohit Sharma

Ashoka Chakra Awardee • Indian Army Para Special Forces Hero

About Major Mohit Sharma

Major Mohit Sharma (13 January 1978 - 21 March 2009) was a highly decorated officer of the Indian Army's elite 1st Battalion, The Parachute Regiment (Special Forces).Known for his courage, intelligence, and leadership, he made the ultimate sacrifice during a counter-terrorism operation in Kupwara, Jammu & Kashmir.

Timeline of His Life

  • 1978 - Born in Rohtak, Haryana.
  • 1995 - Joined the National Defence Academy (NDA).
  • 1999 - Commissioned into the Indian Army from IMA, Dehradun.
  • 2004 - Conducted a daring undercover operation in Kashmir, disguising himself as a militant to gather intelligence.
  • 2009 – On 21 March, led an assault in Kupwara, killing 4 terrorists before laying down his life in the line of duty.
  • 2009 – Posthumously awarded the Ashoka Chakra, India’s highest peacetime gallantry award.

"Men Apart, Every Man an Emperor" — Motto of the Para Special Forces.
