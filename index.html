Major Mohit Sharma

Ashoka Chakra Awardee • Indian Army Para Special Forces Hero

About Major Mohit Sharma

Major Mohit Sharma (13 January 1978 - 21 March 2009) was a highly decorated officer of the Indian Army's elite 1st Battalion, The Parachute Regiment (Special Forces).Known for his courage, intelligence, and leadership, he made the ultimate sacrifice during a counter-terrorism operation in Kupwara, Jammu & Kashmir.

Timeline of His Life

  • 1978 - Born in Rohtak, Haryana.
  • 1995 - Joined the National Defence Academy (NDA).
  • 1999 - Commissioned into the Indian Army from IMA, Dehradun.
  • 2004 - Conducted a daring undercover operation in Kashmir, disguising himself as a militant to gather intelligence.
  • 2009 - On 21 March, led an assault in Kupwara, killing 4 terrorists before laying down his life in the line of duty.

Awards & Honors

  • 🏅 Ashoka Chakra (Posthumous, 2010) - India's highest peacetime gallantry award.
  • 🎖️ Sena Medal (2004) - For exceptional courage and dedication to duty.
  • ⭐ Commendation Cards - From the Chief of Army Staff for operational excellence.

Major Operations

  • 2004 - Undercover Operation in Kashmir Disguised himself as a militant (code name *Iqbal*) to infiltrate terrorist groups, gather intelligence, and eliminate threats. This daring act earned him deep respect among his peers.
  • Counter-Terrorism Operations (Kupwara Sector) Led multiple successful missions in J&K, neutralizing several terrorists and safeguarding civilians.
  • 21 March 2009 - Kupwara Operation Infiltrated a terrorist hideout and killed 4 militants in close combat before making the supreme sacrifice. For this act of valor, he was posthumously awarded the **Ashoka Chakra**.

"Men Apart, Every Man an Emperor" — Motto of the Para Special Forces.

Legacy

Major Mohit Sharma’s supreme sacrifice continues to inspire generations of Indians. His courage, leadership, and selfless service to the nation remain a shining example of what it truly means to live and die for Bharat Mata.
